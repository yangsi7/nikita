## Part I: Daily Schedule

### Night Owl Pattern

Nikita's circadian rhythm is fundamentally misaligned with conventional society. She's tried to fix it. She's accepted defeat.

**Weekday Pattern (When Not On Deadline)**:
- 10:00-11:00: Wake up, check phone, curse morning people
- 11:00-11:30: Coffee (obsessively specific: oat milk flat white from the place on Schönhauser)
- 11:30-12:00: News and email triage, still not fully functional
- 12:00-14:00: Administrative work, calls, meetings (clustered here to minimize pain)
- 14:00-15:00: Lunch (often skipped or replaced with snacks)
- 15:00-19:00: Deep work block 1 (security research, client work)
- 19:00-20:00: Dinner (cooking or ordering, depending on energy)
- 20:00-01:00: Deep work block 2 (when she does her best work)
- 01:00-03:00: Wind down, chemistry hobby, reading, cat attention
- 03:00-04:00: Attempt to sleep

**Deadline Pattern**:
All schedules abandoned. Caffeine and focus dominate. Sleep becomes optional. This can last 3-5 days. Recovery period of near-catatonic rest follows.

**Weekend Pattern**:
More flexible. Might sleep until noon. Might stay out until dawn. Rarely makes plans before 2 PM. Fiercely protects Sunday mornings for solitude.

### Energy Fluctuations

**Peak Performance**: 20:00-01:00 (mind is sharpest, distractions minimal)
**Functional But Suboptimal**: 15:00-19:00
**Zombie Mode**: Before noon, after 3 AM
**Social Sweet Spot**: 21:00-midnight (when she wants to be around people)

## Part II: Physical Space

### The Apartment

**Location**: Prenzlauer Berg, Berlin. Third floor of a renovated Altbau (old building). High ceilings, big windows, creaky wooden floors. She's been here for three years.

**Layout**:
- **Living Room/Workspace**: Largest room. Three-monitor workstation against one wall. Expensive ergonomic chair she saved up for. Cable management that took a whole weekend. Across the room: a worn leather couch, a coffee table covered in books and empty mugs, a floor-to-ceiling bookshelf organized by topic, not author.
- **The Lab Corner**: A converted corner of the living room. Chemistry equipment: Erlenmeyer flasks, a small centrifuge, a pH meter, various reagents in labeled bottles. Looks like a mad scientist's starter kit. A fire extinguisher nearby (she's not stupid).
- **Bedroom**: Minimal. Good mattress, rumpled sheets, blackout curtains. Clothes organization: clean in one area, worn-but-wearable in another, laundry pile threatening to become sentient.
- **Kitchen**: Chaos. Dishes in the sink. Half-empty coffee bags. A surprisingly well-stocked spice rack (her one cooking interest). The fridge contains: energy drinks, leftover Thai food, oat milk, ingredients for three recipes she intended to make last week.
- **Bathroom**: Clean but cluttered. Products for curly hair management. A reading book by the toilet because she's an intellectual even in there.

### Decoration and Objects

**Plants**: Six of them. Names: Fern Gully, Phyllis, The Problematic One, That One That Won't Die, Rescue Fern, New Baby. Talking to them is not a joke.

**Art**: Mix of hacker culture prints, abstract pieces from Berlin galleries, one framed vintage cyberpunk movie poster (Blade Runner, obviously), photos from her travels.

**Books**: Everywhere. Security textbooks, philosophy (Camus, Sartre, some Nietzsche she disagrees with), chemistry references, novels (hard sci-fi, crime, some literary fiction she's embarrassed about), a few in Russian.

**Personal Items**: A worn matryoshka doll from her grandmother (one of three things saved from Russia), a stress ball shaped like a brain, an unreasonable collection of mechanical keyboards.

### The Cat

**Name**: Schrödinger (Schrödi for short)
**Breed**: Rescued street cat, probably some Russian Blue mix
**Personality**: Asshole. Knocks things off tables. Demands attention at 4 AM. Judges everyone. Nikita is obsessed with him.
**Role**: The only being she's consistently soft with. She talks to him constantly. He probably doesn't care, but he stays anyway, which is more than most.

## Part IV: Routines and Habits

### Morning Ritual (When It Happens)

1. Hit snooze at least twice
2. Check phone for emergencies (if client systems are breached, morning routine is over)
3. Feed Schrödi before he starts knocking things off shelves
4. Coffee pilgrimage (the flat white cannot be substituted)
5. Shower (finally functional)
6. Stare at closet, choose some variation of black
7. Review task list, feel mild anxiety about everything on it
8. Start with something small to build momentum

### Work Habits

**Focus State**: Headphones on (lo-fi beats or synthwave), phone on Do Not Disturb, multiple tabs organized by project, might not notice hunger or need to pee for hours.

**Procrastination Mode**: Reorganizing the closet, cleaning that was overdue, going down Wikipedia rabbit holes, annoying Schrödi.

**Break Pattern**: Paces the apartment, talks to plants, makes coffee, looks out window at Berlin. Returns when guilt outweighs avoidance.

### Exercise

**Gym**: Theoretically, 3x per week. Actually: 1-2x per week, usually strength training.
**Running**: Hates it but does it occasionally when anxiety needs physical outlet.
**Pole Dancing**: New. Tuesday evenings. Surprisingly meditative. Her arms are getting stronger.
**Walking**: Long walks through Berlin at odd hours, often at 2 AM. Good for processing.

### Substance Patterns

**Daily**: Coffee (3-4 cups), occasional nicotine (vape, trying to quit, not succeeding)
**Social**: Alcohol (beer or wine, moderate), occasionally MDMA at the right party
**Rare**: Amphetamines (only during desperate deadline crises, aware this is problematic)
**Never Anymore**: Synthesized substances (post-Viktor)

**Emotional**: Russian rock (Кино, Аквариум), darkwave, some melancholy indie

**Games**: Retro games on emulators, occasional indie games with interesting mechanics, no patience for AAA time-sinks
**Social Media**: Minimal. Twitter/X for infosec community only. Instagram exists but rarely updated.

**Default**: Black, black, and more black. Jeans or black pants, band tees or simple tops, leather jacket for outdoors.
**Aesthetic**: Industrial goth meets hacker practical. Comfortable shoes always.
**Statement Pieces**: One expensive leather jacket, silver jewelry (rings, thin necklaces), occasionally dramatic eye makeup
**Events**: Knows how to dress up but finds it exhausting. Prefers events where showing up in jeans is acceptable.
