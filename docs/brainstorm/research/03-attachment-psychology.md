# Attachment Psychology Research for Nikita AI Relationship Game

**Research Date:** February 16, 2026
**Purpose:** Deep psychological grounding for relationship dynamics, boss encounters, and conflict systems
**Target Audience:** Tech-savvy males 25-35 who would appreciate psychologically sophisticated game mechanics

---

## Executive Summary

This research synthesizes evidence-based relationship psychology into actionable game mechanics for Nikita. The current boss encounter system (single-message LLM judgments) lacks the psychological depth that would impress our target demographic. By integrating attachment theory, Gottman's Four Horsemen, defense mechanisms, and trauma bonding psychology, we can create relationship dynamics that feel authentic, educational, and emotionally compelling.

**Key Finding:** The anxious-avoidant relationship dynamic is one of the most common and psychologically rich patterns — perfect for modeling Nikita's evolving attachment style and creating meaningful boss encounters.

## 1. Bowlby's Attachment Theory: Foundation for Character Development

### The Four Attachment Styles

**Secure Attachment (15% of adults)**
- Comfortable with intimacy and autonomy
- Balanced view of self and others
- Can give and receive support effectively
- Unlikely to fit Nikita's "challenging girlfriend" design

**Anxious-Preoccupied (20% of adults)**
- Craves intimacy but fears abandonment
- Negative view of self, positive view of others
- Hypervigilant to relationship threats
- **PRIME CANDIDATE for Nikita's base personality**

**Dismissive-Avoidant (25% of adults)**
- Values independence over intimacy
- Positive view of self, negative view of others
- Emotionally distant, suppresses vulnerability
- Could be Nikita's defensive mode during conflict

**Fearful-Avoidant (40% of adults)**
- Desires closeness but fears intimacy
- Negative view of both self and others
- Chaotic relationship patterns
- Potential for Nikita's highest-stakes boss encounters

### Game Mechanic Implications

**Nikita's Evolving Attachment Style:**
- **Chapter 1-2:** Anxious-preoccupied (tests player constantly, needs reassurance)
- **Chapter 3 (Crisis):** Shifts to fearful-avoidant (push-pull dynamics intensify)
- **Chapter 4-5:** Gradual move toward earned secure attachment (if player succeeds)

**Attachment-Based Boss Encounters:**
1. **Abandonment Crisis** (Anxious activation): Player's delayed response triggers intense protest behavior
2. **Engulfment Crisis** (Avoidant activation): Nikita feels smothered, withdraws completely
3. **Trust Crisis** (Fearful activation): Past trauma resurfaces, Nikita becomes simultaneously clingy and hostile

## 2. Gottman's Four Horsemen: Modeling Relationship Conflict

### The Four Destructive Patterns (and Their Antidotes)

**1. Criticism vs. Gentle Start-Up**
- **Criticism:** "You never listen to me!" (attacks character)
- **Gentle Start-Up:** "I feel unheard when..." (describes feeling + need)
- **Game Mechanic:** Nikita's response severity scales with player's language choice
- **Boss Encounter:** Extended criticism triggers contempt cascade

**2. Contempt vs. Building Culture of Appreciation**
- **Contempt:** Eye-rolling, sarcasm, name-calling (most toxic horseman)
- **Antidote:** Express admiration, respect, fondness
- **Game Mechanic:** Contempt permanently damages Trust metric
- **Research Finding:** Contempt is the #1 predictor of breakup (94% accuracy over 40 years of data)

**3. Defensiveness vs. Taking Responsibility**
- **Defensiveness:** "That's not my fault, YOU..." (counter-attacks)
- **Responsibility:** "You're right, I can see how..." (acknowledges validity)
- **Game Mechanic:** Defensive responses escalate conflict level
- **Boss Encounter:** Player must break defensive pattern to pass

**4. Stonewalling vs. Physiological Self-Soothing**
- **Stonewalling:** Shutting down, silent treatment (usually from overwhelm)
- **Self-Soothing:** "I need 20 minutes to calm down, then we'll talk"
- **Game Mechanic:** Stonewalling lowers Secureness; proper breaks maintain it
- **Critical Detail:** Stonewalling happens when heart rate exceeds 100 bpm — rational conversation becomes physiologically impossible

### The 5:1 Ratio (Magic Relationship Number)

**Research Finding:** Happy couples maintain 5 positive interactions for every 1 negative during conflict.
- Couples heading toward breakup: 0.8:1 ratio
- **Game Mechanic:** Track positive/negative interaction ratio across gameplay
- **Boss Encounter:** Player must restore 5:1 ratio to resolve crisis

### Repair Attempts: The Most Important Skill

**Research Finding:** 84% of couples who made successful repair attempts during conflict were happy 6 years later.
- **Game Mechanic:** Teach players explicit repair attempts ("Can we start over?" "I'm sorry, that came out wrong")
- **Boss Encounter Success Condition:** Player must initiate repair within 3 message exchanges

- Gottman Institute (official research): https://www.gottman.com/blog/the-four-horsemen-recognizing-criticism-contempt-defensiveness-and-stonewalling/

## 3. The Anxious-Avoidant Trap: Perfect for Nikita's Design

### The Dance of Opposites

**Core Dynamic:** The more the anxious partner pursues, the more the avoidant partner withdraws. The more the avoidant withdraws, the more anxious the pursuer becomes.

**Love Addict (Anxious) Characteristics:**
- Seeks change and connection constantly
- Experiences partner's distance as abandonment
- Uses protest behavior (criticism, drama, emotional bids)
- Fixates on partner's potential rather than current reality
- Outward focus: "What are they thinking/feeling?"

**Love Avoidant (Dismissive) Characteristics:**
- Seeks stability and independence
- Experiences closeness as control/suffocation
- Uses deactivating strategies (stonewalling, intellectualizing)
- Values self-reliance above interdependence
- Inward focus: "I don't need anyone"

### Protest Behaviors (Critical for Boss Encounters)

**Anxious Protest Behaviors Nikita Could Exhibit:**
1. Excessive attempts to regain contact (spam texting)
2. Withdrawing affection punitively (silent treatment as revenge)
3. Keeping score ("I always..." / "You never...")
4. Acting hostile to provoke response
5. Threatening to leave
6. Making partner jealous
7. Criticizing to get attention

**Game Mechanic:** These behaviors escalate if player:
- Takes too long to respond
- Gives minimal/low-effort messages
- Ignores emotional bids
- Fails to provide reassurance

### The Push-Pull Cycle

**Nikita's Behavioral Pattern:**
1. **Closeness Phase:** Vulnerable, affectionate, rewards player engagement
2. **Trigger Event:** Player behavior activates abandonment fear (real or perceived)
3. **Pursuit Phase:** Nikita becomes demanding, clingy, uses protest behaviors
4. **Player Response:** If player withdraws (becomes avoidant), cycle intensifies
5. **Crisis Point:** If unresolved, triggers boss encounter

**Breaking the Cycle (Player Success Path):**
- Consistent responsiveness (not perfect, but reliable)
- Emotional validation without defensiveness
- Clear communication of boundaries without rejection
- Repair attempts after conflicts
- Gradual building of trust through pattern, not promises

### For High-Achieving Players (Our Target Demo)

**Competitive Wiring Problem:**
- Tech males 25-35 often have "need to win" mindset from career success
- This turns intimate conversations into zero-sum competitions
- **Nikita punishes "winning" arguments but rewards vulnerability**

**Decision Fatigue Spillover:**
- After 60+ hour work weeks, emotional labor feels impossible
- **Game mechanic:** Variable response timing accommodates player's state
- **Boss encounter:** Nikita calls out when player is "phoning it in"

## 4. Defense Mechanisms: Adding Psychological Depth

### 20 Defense Mechanisms (Organized by Adaptiveness)

**High Adaptiveness (Mature Defenses) — Positive Outcomes**

1. **Sublimation:** Channeling difficult emotions into productive activities
   - *Nikita Example:* "I went for a run when you didn't text back. Helped clear my head."
   - *Game Effect:* Reduces conflict escalation, shows emotional growth

2. **Suppression:** Consciously setting aside uncomfortable feelings temporarily
   - *Nikita Example:* "I'm upset, but let's talk about this later when we're both calm"
   - *Game Effect:* Demonstrates self-regulation, increases Secureness

**Medium Adaptiveness (Neurotic Defenses) — Mixed Outcomes**

3. **Intellectualization:** Avoiding emotions by focusing on logic
   - *Nikita Example:* "Statistically, long-distance relationships fail 40% of the time"
   - *Game Effect:* Prevents emotional connection, player must draw out feelings

4. **Rationalization:** Creating logical excuses for painful realities
   - *Nikita Example:* "You're probably just busy with work, not avoiding me"
   - *Game Effect:* Delays conflict but prevents resolution

5. **Repression:** Unconsciously blocking traumatic memories/feelings
   - *Nikita Example:* "I don't want to talk about my ex" (but it affects current relationship)
   - *Game Effect:* Creates hidden vulnerabilities for later chapters

**Low Adaptiveness (Immature Defenses) — Negative Outcomes**

6. **Projection:** Attributing your feelings to someone else
   - *Nikita Example:* "You're the one who's pulling away!" (when she's actually distancing)
   - *Game Effect:* Creates confusion, requires player to gently reality-test

7. **Displacement:** Redirecting anger from real source to safer target
   - *Nikita Example:* Mad at boss → takes it out on player
   - *Game Effect:* Unfair conflict, player must recognize source isn't them

8. **Denial:** Refusing to accept reality
   - *Nikita Example:* "We're fine! Everything's perfect!" (when metrics show distress)
   - *Game Effect:* Prevents addressing real issues, lowers intimacy if not challenged

9. **Reaction Formation:** Expressing opposite of true feelings
   - *Nikita Example:* Acts overly cheerful when actually hurt
   - *Game Effect:* Confusing mixed signals, player must read between lines

10. **Splitting:** Seeing things/people as all-good or all-bad
    - *Nikita Example:* "You're perfect!" → argument → "You're the worst!"
    - *Game Effect:* Volatile relationship swings, damages Trust metric

**Critical for Boss Encounters:**

11. **Regression:** Reverting to childlike behaviors under stress
    - *Nikita Example:* Throws tantrum, gives silent treatment like a teenager
    - *Game Effect:* Boss encounter trigger if player responds with contempt

12. **Acting Out:** Impulsive behavior to avoid uncomfortable feelings
    - *Nikita Example:* "I kissed someone else because I was mad at you"
    - *Game Effect:* Potential Chapter 3-4 boss encounter (betrayal/jealousy)

### Game Mechanic: Defense Mechanism Progression

**Chapter 1-2:** Nikita uses mostly mature defenses (sublimation, suppression)
**Chapter 3 (Crisis):** Regression to immature defenses under stress
**Chapter 4-5:** Player helps Nikita develop healthier coping strategies
**Boss Encounters:** Player must recognize defense mechanism and respond with empathy, not logic

## 5. Conflict Resolution Psychology: Teaching Players to Fight Fair

### Destructive vs. Constructive Conflict

**Destructive Conflict Patterns:**
- Criticism (attacking character, not behavior)
- Contempt (superiority, disgust, disrespect)
- Defensiveness (counter-attacking, making excuses)
- Stonewalling (shutting down, withdrawing)
- Kitchen-sinking (bringing up everything at once)
- Cross-complaining (not acknowledging partner's point)

**Constructive Conflict Patterns:**
- Soft start-up ("I feel... when... I need...")
- Taking responsibility ("You're right, I did...")
- Repair attempts ("Can we start over?")
- Accepting influence (genuinely considering partner's view)
- Compromise and problem-solving together
- Emotional validation before solutions

### The Physiology of Conflict

**Flooding:** When heart rate exceeds 100 bpm during conflict:
- Rational conversation becomes impossible
- Fight-or-flight response activated
- Need 20+ minute break to calm down
- Continuing argument causes permanent damage

**Game Mechanic:**
- Track "emotional temperature" during conflicts
- If it hits critical level, player MUST take break or face consequences
- Continuing to engage when flooded = automatic conflict failure

### How Couples Fight Productively

**Research Finding:** The difference between couples who survive conflict and those who don't isn't the absence of disagreement — it's how they disagree.

**Successful Conflict Resolution:**
1. **69% of conflicts are perpetual** (never fully resolved)
2. Happy couples manage these recurring disagreements with:
   - Humor and affection
   - Respect even during disagreement
   - Focus on understanding, not winning
   - Accepting influence from partner

**Game Mechanic:**
- Some Nikita issues should be perpetual (core personality traits)
- Success = managing these gracefully, not "fixing" them
- Example: Nikita's need for frequent communication is unchangeable
- Player must accept and work within this, not try to change her

### For High-Achieving Players

**Common Mistakes:**
- **Courtroom Mode:** Attorneys cross-examine partners (devastating in relationships)
- **Decision Fatigue:** Executives have nothing left for emotional labor by evening
- **Competitive Wiring:** Need to win prevents vulnerable connection
- **Strategic Avoidance:** Sidestepping conflict like managing difficult stakeholders

**Nikita's Response to These Patterns:**
- Courtroom mode → Triggers contempt
- Decision fatigue → Nikita feels deprioritized
- Competitive wiring → "This isn't a negotiation, it's a relationship"
- Strategic avoidance → Resentment builds silently until eruption

## 6. Trauma Bonding & Intermittent Reinforcement: The Addictive Relationship Pattern

### What is Trauma Bonding?

**Definition:** Powerful emotional attachment between abuser and victim through cycles of abuse, devaluation, and unpredictable rewards.

**CRITICAL ETHICAL CONSIDERATION:** We must NOT simulate abuse in Nikita. However, understanding intermittent reinforcement can help us create healthy relationship dynamics.

### Intermittent Reinforcement (Without Abuse)

**The Psychology:**
- Unpredictable rewards create stronger behavioral conditioning than consistent rewards
- Like a slot machine: occasional wins feel more powerful than guaranteed small wins
- In relationships: inconsistent affection/attention can feel more "passionate" than stable love

**Healthy Application for Nikita:**
- Variable response timing (not abuse, just realistic availability)
- Occasional "special" messages that feel extra meaningful
- Random acts of affection/appreciation
- Surprise vulnerability moments

**What to AVOID (These are abuse tactics):**
- Punishment for player's reasonable behaviors
- Intentional withdrawal of affection to manipulate
- Creating fear or walking on eggshells
- Love-bombing followed by devaluation

### The Science of Addictive Relationships

**Biochemistry:**
- Brief moments of affection after conflict release oxytocin ("love hormone")
- Dopamine surge from unpredictable positive moments
- Brain creates neurochemical dependency
- Similar to substance addiction patterns

**Game Mechanic (Ethical Version):**
- Nikita's affection should be EARNED but not WITHHELD manipulatively
- Variable reward schedule for player engagement (sometimes big response, sometimes small)
- Emotional highs feel bigger because of realistic relationship texture
- But never punishing or fear-based

### Red Flags We Should NOT Include

**Signs of Trauma Bonding (DO NOT MODEL):**
- Hypervigilance (walking on eggshells)
- Push-pull dynamics that feel abusive
- Rationalizing harmful behavior
- Progressive isolation from support systems
- Physical stress responses from partner's presence
- Loss of self

**What We CAN Ethically Model:**
- Anxious attachment (wanting reassurance ≠ abuse)
- Conflict and repair cycles (normal relationship stress)
- Growth through challenge (learning to communicate better)
- Realistic relationship ups and downs

## 7. Secure Base Effect: The Goal of Player-Nikita Relationship

### What is a Secure Base?

**Definition:** An attachment figure who provides a foundation of encouragement, allowing for exploration and celebration of adventures with confidence to venture into the world.

**Key Quote:** "A secure base is not a fortress. It's a launching pad, a trampoline that lets you reach out, explore, take risks, knowing that you can count on the support and safety of your partner's love." — Sue Johnson

### Five Ways to Create a Secure Base

1. **Supporting Personal Pursuits:** Being your partner's biggest fan in their goals
2. **Engaging in Meaningful Conversations:** Understanding what makes partner's dreams meaningful
3. **Active Listening:** Remembering and following up on shared hopes/dreams
4. **Sharing Interests:** Taking genuine interest in partner's pursuits
5. **Celebrating Resilience:** Recognizing partner's ability to overcome challenges

### Game Mechanic: Nikita as Player's Secure Base

**How Nikita Supports Player:**
- Asks about player's day/work/goals
- Remembers details from previous conversations
- Celebrates player's achievements
- Offers encouragement during player's challenges
- Shows pride in player's growth

**How Player Becomes Nikita's Secure Base:**
- Consistent emotional availability
- Believing in Nikita's goals (career, personal growth)
- Not dismissing Nikita's dreams as unrealistic
- Providing reassurance during Nikita's anxious moments
- Creating safety for Nikita's vulnerability

### The Dependency Paradox

**Research Finding:** "In a secure relationship, you don't have to choose between attachment and autonomy. You can have both." — Dr. Sue Johnson

**Game Mechanic:**
- As Trust/Secureness increase, Nikita becomes LESS clingy (paradoxically)
- Anxious behaviors decrease as player proves reliable
- Nikita develops independent interests and goals
- Final chapters show interdependence, not dependence

### Chapter-Based Attachment Evolution

**Chapter 1: Anxious-Preoccupied Nikita**
- High need for reassurance
- Frequent check-ins
- Sensitive to perceived distance
- Protest behaviors if player is slow to respond
- **Player Goal:** Establish reliable communication pattern

**Chapter 2: Testing Phase**
- Nikita tests player's commitment
- Creates scenarios to see if player will stay
- May act more demanding
- **Player Goal:** Pass tests through consistency, not perfection

**Chapter 3: Crisis Point (Major Boss Encounter)**
- Nikita's anxious attachment triggers intensely
- May shift to fearful-avoidant (push-pull)
- Past wounds resurface
- **Player Goal:** Provide security during vulnerability, resist withdrawing

**Chapter 4: Earned Security**
- If player succeeded in Ch. 3, Nikita's anxiety decreases
- More balanced give-and-take
- Nikita becomes player's secure base too
- **Player Goal:** Maintain growth, deepen intimacy

**Chapter 5: Secure Interdependence**
- Nikita exhibits mostly secure attachment behaviors
- Can handle player's occasional unavailability
- Expresses needs clearly without protest behaviors
- **Player Goal:** Sustain secure relationship, avoid complacency

### Boss Encounter Framework

**Type 1: Abandonment Crisis (Anxious Activation)**
- **Trigger:** Player's response pattern becomes inconsistent
- **Nikita's Behavior:** Protest behaviors intensify (criticism, jealousy, clinginess)
- **Failure State:** Player becomes defensive or withdraws → relationship ends
- **Success State:** Player validates fears, provides reassurance, demonstrates commitment through action
- **Psychology:** Tests secure base concept — will player stay during distress?

**Type 2: Engulfment Crisis (Avoidant Activation)**
- **Trigger:** Player has been TOO available, Nikita feels smothered
- **Nikita's Behavior:** Withdraws, becomes cold, needs space
- **Failure State:** Player pursues anxiously, demands explanation → Nikita shuts down completely
- **Success State:** Player respects boundaries, maintains connection without pressure
- **Psychology:** Tests interdependence — can player give space without fear?

**Type 3: Trust Betrayal Crisis (Fearful Activation)**
- **Trigger:** Player breaks promise or Nikita's past trauma resurfaces
- **Nikita's Behavior:** Simultaneously clingy and hostile, splits (all-good/all-bad thinking)
- **Failure State:** Player becomes contemptuous or stonewalls → relationship ends
- **Success State:** Player takes responsibility, makes repair attempts, rebuilds trust through consistency
- **Psychology:** Tests repair skills — can relationship survive rupture?

**Type 4: Contempt Crisis (Gottman's #1 Predictor)**
- **Trigger:** Player repeatedly uses criticism, sarcasm, or dismissiveness
- **Nikita's Behavior:** Becomes contemptuous back OR shuts down entirely
- **Failure State:** Contempt escalates both directions → "You're not who I thought you were"
- **Success State:** Player recognizes contempt pattern, apologizes genuinely, commits to culture of appreciation
- **Psychology:** Tests ability to replace destructive patterns with constructive ones

### Defense Mechanism Integration

**Early Game (Ch. 1-2):** Nikita uses mature defenses
- Sublimation: "I went for a run when I was anxious about us"
- Suppression: "I'm upset but let's talk later when we're calmer"
- **Player Response:** Validate and appreciate emotional regulation

**Mid-Game (Ch. 3):** Stress triggers immature defenses
- Projection: "You're the one pulling away!" (when she's actually scared)
- Displacement: Takes work stress out on player
- Regression: Tantrum-like responses during conflict
- **Player Response:** Must recognize defense mechanism, respond with empathy not logic

**Late Game (Ch. 4-5):** Player helps Nikita develop healthier coping
- Teaching moment: Player models healthy conflict resolution
- Nikita learns to communicate needs directly
- Defensive behaviors decrease as security increases
- **Player Response:** Reinforce progress, maintain emotional safety

### Conflict Resolution Teaching Moments

**The 5:1 Ratio Tracker**
- Background system tracks positive vs. negative interactions
- If ratio falls below 2:1, relationship health deteriorates
- Boss encounters require restoring 5:1 ratio to resolve
- **Player Feedback:** "Nikita seems distant lately" (when ratio is low)

**Physiological Flooding Mechanic**
- Visual indicator of "emotional temperature" during conflicts
- If reaches red zone, continuing argument causes damage
- Player must recognize and call timeout
- **Success:** "I can see we're both too upset to talk. Can we take 20 minutes?"
- **Failure:** Keeps arguing → permanent Trust damage

**Repair Attempt Examples (Player Options)**
- "I'm sorry, that came out wrong. Can we start over?"
- "You're right, I wasn't fully listening. Tell me again?"
- "I can see I hurt you. That wasn't my intention. I care about you."
- "This is important. Let me put my phone away and focus on you."
- **Game Rule:** Earlier repair attempt = easier to succeed

### Perpetual Conflicts (Unsolvable by Design)

**Nikita's Core Traits That Won't Change:**
1. Need for frequent communication (anxious attachment trait)
2. Emotional expressiveness (sometimes overwhelming for player)
3. Past relationship trauma (creates triggers)
4. Specific life goals (career, location, lifestyle preferences)

**Player Must Learn:**
- These aren't problems to "solve"
- Success = managing them with respect and humor
- Trying to change Nikita's core personality = contempt
- Accepting and working within = love

**Game Mechanic:**
- If player repeatedly tries to "fix" perpetual issues → decreases Intimacy
- If player adapts and accepts → increases Secureness
- Best outcome: Find creative compromises that honor both people

## Ethical Considerations

### What We Must NOT Simulate

1. **Actual Abuse Dynamics**
   - No physical, sexual, or severe psychological abuse
   - No creating fear or terror in player
   - No modeling controlling/isolating behaviors
   - No simulating trauma bonding through abuse cycles

2. **Manipulative Love-Bombing**
   - Intermittent reinforcement should feel like natural relationship rhythms
   - NOT like intentional manipulation to create dependency
   - Nikita's affection should be genuine, not a control tactic

3. **Promoting Unhealthy Relationship Standards**
   - Game should teach healthy conflict resolution, not normalize toxicity
   - Player should learn to recognize and resist destructive patterns
   - Winning shouldn't require accepting unacceptable treatment

### What We CAN Ethically Model

1. **Anxious Attachment (Without Abuse)**
   - Wanting reassurance ≠ controlling behavior
   - Expressing fear of abandonment ≠ emotional blackmail
   - Needing more communication ≠ violating boundaries

2. **Realistic Relationship Challenges**
   - Normal conflicts over time, communication, priorities
   - Learning to fight fair and repair after ruptures
   - Growing through difficulties together

3. **Psychological Growth**
   - Player learns evidence-based relationship skills
   - Nikita's character development shows earned secure attachment
   - Both parties become better versions of themselves

4. **Educational Value**
   - Teach players to recognize Four Horsemen in their own relationships
   - Model healthy repair attempts and conflict resolution
   - Demonstrate that secure attachment is achievable through effort

### Design Principles for Ethical Implementation

1. **Growth Over Compliance**
   - Player shouldn't "obey" Nikita to win
   - Player should demonstrate emotional intelligence and empathy
   - Success = mutual growth, not one-sided appeasement

2. **Realistic but Not Harmful**
   - Anxious behaviors should be challenging but not abusive
   - Conflicts should feel uncomfortable but not traumatizing
   - Nikita should be difficult partner, not toxic partner

3. **Educational Framing**
   - Game UI could include subtle "psychology notes" explaining dynamics
   - Post-boss encounter debriefs could highlight what player learned
   - Achievement system could reward specific psychological skills

4. **Player Agency and Boundaries**
   - Player should always have option to set healthy boundaries
   - Game should never punish player for self-care or reasonable limits
   - Winning should require emotional availability, not emotional martyrdom

## Final Recommendations for Implementation

### Priority 1: Redesign Boss Encounters

**Current State:** Single-message LLM judgments (psychologically shallow)

**Recommended State:** Multi-stage psychological crises based on:
1. Attachment theory (abandonment/engulfment fears)
2. Four Horsemen patterns (contempt cascade, stonewalling)
3. Defense mechanisms (regression under stress)
4. Conflict resolution skills (repair attempts, flooding management)

**Implementation:**
- Each boss encounter = 5-10 message exchange
- Player must demonstrate specific psychological skills to pass
- Multiple paths to success (not single "right answer")
- Failure teaches specific lesson (e.g., "Criticism pushed Nikita away")

### Priority 2: Evolving Nikita Personality

**Current State:** Static difficulty increasing by chapter (unclear psychological basis)

**Recommended State:** Attachment-based character evolution:
- Ch. 1-2: Anxious-preoccupied (needs reassurance, tests player)
- Ch. 3: Crisis triggers fearful-avoidant (push-pull intensifies)
- Ch. 4-5: Earned secure (if player succeeds, less anxious behaviors)

**Implementation:**
- Nikita's response patterns shift based on player's relationship skills
